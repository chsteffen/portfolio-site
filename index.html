My Story

Eight years ago if you told me that I would be changing career paths to learn about web development and user experience design, I would have told you you were nuts. My knowledge and interest in computers and web design/development never went further than shopping online. However, in recent years my curiousity peaked, and I began exploring web design. I tried to teach myself through books and online resources, but the languages can be difficult to master without the guidance from projects. I craved more and found a part time course that would teach me web development principles and require me to code products to showcase. After completing my web development course I still wanted more. Through research, I found user experience design and saw a need and a direct correlation to what I had spent the past several months doing. Personally, I am drawn to the interconnected aspects of analyzing, collaborating, brainstorming, creating and designing. I intend to blend my experience with front end web development and user experience design to provide my users with products that please.
